WHO Guideline Standards Thresholds

Under WHO and EPA secondary drinking water regulations, the chemical parameters are subject to the following standard bounds:

  • pH Level (6.5 – 8.5): Dictates corrosion or scaling behavior. Acidic water dissolves pipe metals; alkaline water forms scale deposits.
  • Dissolved Solids (0 – 1000 ppm): Total dissolved minerals. Values above 1000 ppm indicate excessive scaling, mineral taste, and potential laxative effects.
  • Chloramines (0.0 – 4.0 ppm): Disinfectant levels. Exceeding 4.0 ppm can lead to aesthetic changes, skin irritation, and toxic by-products.
  • Sulfate (0 – 250 mg/L): Naturally occurring salts. Concentrations above 250 mg/L cause laxative effects and mineral tastes.
  • Turbidity (0.0 – 5.0 NTU): Clarity measure. Readings above 5.0 NTU shelter pathogens and disrupt disinfection.

        Architectural Rationale

        XGBoost was selected as the final classifier based on:

        • Nonlinear Splitting: Safely mapping chemical bounds (which do not exhibit simple linear correlation relative to potability) requires multi-split decision boundaries.
        • Training weights: Accommodates sample adjustments directly in optimization steps to normalize class disparity labels.
        • TreeExplainer integrations: Fast, exact calculation of Shapley additive contribution margins for immediate visual explanations.

        Platform & Methodology Information

        Explainable AI via Shapley Values

        Shapley values originate from cooperative game theory, determining the marginal contribution of each player (feature) to the game's outcome (prediction). In our pipeline, the SHAP values detail exactly how much each parameter (pH, Sulfate, TDS, etc.) increased or decreased the classification margin relative to a baseline value. This ensures full auditing safety and replaces standard "black-box" model outputs.

        Mathematical Safety Index Scoring

        While the machine learning model classifies potability based on a high-dimensional decision boundary trained on real historical water samples, the Water Safety Score represents a strict, rule-based mathematical index. It applies penalties for any parameter deviating from standard WHO standards, scaling from 0 (extreme health risk) to 100 (fully safe, zero chemical deviations). This dual-metric pipeline yields both empirical predictions and mathematical compliance margins.

        Disclaimer and Limitations

        All safety recommendations generated by Google's Gemini generative model or our fallback rules engine are for informational, diagnostic, and laboratory study purposes. Real water safety requires physical testing, chemical titration, and micro-biological cultures. Ensure standard lab audits are conducted before certifying public water supplies.
